About me

When I was 4 years old, my dad let me play games on his office computer, a new Windows XP machine. Granted, calls on our home phone and using the dial-up internet were mutually exclusive activities and my parents were chatty; I had to make do with all the offline content I could. And thanks to my dad's software CD collection, I did. I made my dream home 100 times over with home design modelers, played educational titles about inventors and historical periods, and messed around with as many Windows features as I could. The last one was important because the dial-up only connected when you logged into the full-screen browser, MSN, and parental controls prevented me from minimizing the window or accessing anything outside MSN content. One day, screwing around, I found out that I could run the dial-up without booting up the browser by trying to access a help article in the login screen. Then, I could open Internet Explorer and bypass all the parental locks. I was now a 5 year old with unrestricted access to the 2003 internet. The rest is history.
